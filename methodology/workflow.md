# The research pipeline

One research cycle proceeds through ten stations, organized in five layers.

| Layer | Stations |
|---|---|
| **Human researcher** | Sets the standards and evaluation criteria; makes the final decisions. Decision requests reach the human with full context and the expected impact of each option. |
| **Coordinator** | 1. Select the topic from a ranked list of open questions. 2. Write the study contract: hypotheses and pass criteria, fixed before any run. 9. Compile the lessons after review. |
| **Research agent** | 3. Search the laboratory archive and declare which verified textbook facts the work will be checked against, before designing. 4. Design and simulate on switched-circuit physics; enumerate exhaustively whenever the state space allows. 5. Run self-checks — every pass/fail criterion must first be shown capable of failing — and record the design choices considered and rejected. |
| **Review agents** | 6. The first reviewer re-runs all code and independently recomputes every reported number with its own implementation. 7. The second reviewer examines the interpretation: recounting, waveform comparison, build-versus-specification checks, precedent and textbook checks, consistency of abstraction levels, and completeness of edge cases. |
| **Permanent records** | Experiment archive (including failures), knowledge graph, rule book with automated tests, provenance graph. |

Working rules that apply throughout:

- Numbers are never invented. Every fact carries its source; every
  measurement carries its artifact.
- A claim is accepted only after surviving independent re-derivation.
- Errors remain on the record, together with their corrections.
- A conclusion may only claim the conditions that were actually tested.
- Every acceptance criterion must be demonstrated to be capable of
  failing; a check that always passes provides no evidence.
